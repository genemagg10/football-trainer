What we believe

Great players are grown, not picked.

Every drill, video, and recommendation here is filtered through a single question: does this help a young player fall in love with the game while becoming a better, healthier athlete? We adapt the best of England's Player DNA, Germany's DFB curriculum, Spain's La Masia ethos, the Dutch TIPS model, and France's Clairefontaine method — and translate them into clear guidance for parents and coaches.

Stage-appropriate

Right thing, right time

What a 7-year-old needs (free play, ball mastery, joy) is the opposite of what a 15-year-old needs (positional play, periodization, tactical IQ). We map every drill and concept to a developmental window.

Boys & girls

Different, on purpose

Girls hit puberty 1–2 years earlier than boys, are 3–6× more likely to suffer ACL injuries, and respond differently to feedback styles. We highlight the differences instead of pretending they don't exist.

Individual & team

Player first, team second

A team is the byproduct of well-developed individuals. Each stage shows which minutes belong to me with the ball, and which belong to us moving together.

Cited & translated

Sources you can verify

Every recommendation links back to FA, DFB, RFEF, KNVB, FIFA, peer-reviewed research, or named coach education materials — translated into English where needed.

Health first

Long-term athlete development

Built on the LTAD framework, with bio-banding, ACL prevention, concussion guidance, and burnout awareness baked in — not bolted on.

Inclusive

Every kid, every body

Late developers, early bloomers, neurodivergent players, disability football — youth soccer is for everyone. We address Relative Age Effect head-on.

A clean roadmap from "first touch" to "first contract." Each stage covers physical, technical, tactical, psychological, and social development — with featured drills from the top European academies.

European philosophies

Five traditions, one common thread.

No country has a monopoly on great development. We borrow England's clarity, Germany's structure, Spain's possession ideals, the Dutch obsession with technique, and the French academy production line.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 England — The FA Player DNA

Five core ingredients: technical, tactical, physical, psychological, social. England has rebuilt a development system around "in possession, out of possession, transitions".

🇩🇪 Germany — The DFB curriculum

Built after Euro 2000 humiliation. Talent centers, standardized coach education, and the "learn through play" Funino format.

🇪🇸 Spain — La Masia & the rondo

Ball circulation as a religion. Positional play, intelligent pressing, and the rondo as the daily warm-up of choice from age 6 to the first team.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — TIPS & Total Football

Technique, Insight, Personality, Speed. Every player learns every position. Coerver Coaching grew here for a reason.

Beyond the brief

What we add that most sites miss.

If we want to actually help the youth soccer community, we have to talk about the stuff most curricula leave out.

🧬 Bio-banding

Group players by maturation, not birthday. A 13-year-old who's already 6'1" and a 13-year-old who's 5'2" need different sessions — and shouldn't be cut from the same tryout.

📅 Relative Age Effect

Up to 50% of academy intakes are January–March born. We show coaches how to spot — and stop — selection bias.

🦵 ACL prevention for girls

Female players are 3–6× more likely to tear an ACL. The FIFA 11+ Kids and PEP programs cut that risk in half. Every girls' team should run them.

🧠 Concussion & heading

US Soccer prohibits heading before U12; the FA recommends it sparingly to U18. We follow the science, not tradition.

😮‍💨 Burnout & mental health

Early specialization is a top predictor of dropout and injury. We advocate multi-sport play through age 12 and rest as a training input.

🍎 Fueling young athletes

Pre/post-training nutrition, sleep, hydration, iron status (especially for menstruating players). Recovery is training.

🥅 Goalkeeper track

GKs are footballers first. Stage-appropriate handling, footwork, and distribution — without isolating them from the squad.

👨‍👩‍👧 Parent code of conduct

The single biggest variable in a kid's enjoyment is the car ride home. We give parents the words.

♿ Inclusive football

Disability football, neurodivergent players, mixed-gender play guidelines. Soccer should make space for every kid.
